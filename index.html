Introduction

Cryptocurrencies exhibit fundamentally different behavior compared to traditional stock markets, particularly evident during pivotal events such as the COVID-19 pandemic, the 2021 bull run, the 2022-2023 market correction, and the Terra-LUNA and FTX collapses. While the S&P 500 demonstrates consistent, predictable patterns rooted in established economic fundamentals, digital assets like Bitcoin, Ethereum, XRP, Litecoin, and BNB experience dramatically accelerated volatility cycles. The correlation between these asset classes remains dynamic—at times moving in tandem, at others diverging completely—raising critical questions about market integration and systemic risk exposure.

Our research addresses the growing imperative to understand crypto-equity market dynamics as digital assets mature into a multi-trillion dollar asset class. With institutional adoption accelerating, regulatory frameworks evolving, and macroeconomic policy increasingly impacting both traditional and digital markets, the intersection of these financial ecosystems demands rigorous analysis. This project leverages advanced data visualization techniques to illuminate whether cryptocurrencies are converging toward traditional market behavior or maintaining their status as an independent, alternative asset class with distinct risk-return characteristics.

Research Objectives

This analysis employs quantitative methods and interactive visualizations to investigate three fundamental questions:

  • Volatility Analysis: Quantifying the magnitude of price fluctuations in major cryptocurrencies relative to the S&P 500 across different market regimes
  • Correlation Dynamics: Identifying temporal shifts in asset correlation patterns and determining when crypto markets decouple from or synchronize with equity markets
  • Event-Driven Behavior: Isolating specific macro events that trigger significant regime changes in market behavior and cross-asset relationships

Existing academic literature demonstrates that crypto-equity correlations are non-stationary, intensifying during market stress while weakening in stable conditions. Our visualization-driven approach provides intuitive access to these complex relationships, enabling viewers to observe when cryptocurrencies function as high-beta equity proxies versus when they behave as uncorrelated alternative investments with unique risk factors.

Data Description

Our comprehensive dataset spans five years of daily market data (2020-2025) sourced via the yfinance API, encompassing six strategically selected assets: SPY (S&P 500 ETF), Bitcoin (BTC), Ethereum (ETH), XRP, Litecoin (LTC), and Binance Coin (BNB). This selection represents a cross-section of market capitalizations, use cases, and institutional adoption levels within the crypto ecosystem.

SPY

SPDR S&P 500 ETF Trust — the benchmark representing large-cap U.S. equity market performance and traditional finance.

BTC

Bitcoin — the dominant cryptocurrency by market capitalization, serving as the digital gold standard and primary crypto market benchmark.

ETH

Ethereum — the leading smart contract platform, representing programmable blockchain infrastructure and decentralized applications.

XRP

Ripple — a payments-focused digital asset with significant exposure to regulatory developments and institutional cross-border settlement use cases.

LTC

Litecoin — an established cryptocurrency optimized for transaction speed and efficiency, often viewed as Bitcoin's "silver" to its "gold."

BNB

Binance Coin — the native token of the Binance exchange ecosystem, heavily influenced by centralized exchange dynamics and platform developments.

The raw dataset captures essential OHLCV data (Open, High, Low, Close, Volume) alongside adjusted closing prices to account for corporate actions. Following rigorous data cleaning protocols—including duplicate removal, datetime normalization, and null value imputation—we augmented the dataset with a "Drivers" column that maps each asset to its underlying organizations or protocols, providing contextual depth for interpreting market movements.

Our engineered feature set transforms this raw data into analytically powerful metrics through sophisticated temporal aggregation. The final analytical dataset contains over 10,000 daily observations, structured to enable both cross-sectional and time-series analysis across all six assets simultaneously.

Advanced Feature Engineering

To support comprehensive market behavior analysis, we derived the following quantitative features:

  • Daily Returns: Period-over-period percentage changes in closing prices, capturing immediate price momentum
  • Log Returns: Continuously compounded returns providing superior statistical properties for long-term analysis and volatility modeling
  • Rolling Volatility (30-day): Standard deviation of returns over sliding windows, quantifying market uncertainty and risk exposure
  • Rolling Mean (30-day): Moving averages that filter high-frequency noise to reveal underlying price trends and momentum
  • Rolling Maximum: Cumulative peak prices enabling drawdown calculations and trend identification
  • Maximum Drawdown: Peak-to-trough decline metrics measuring maximum capital at risk during adverse market conditions
  • SPY Correlation (30-day rolling): Dynamic correlation coefficients tracking the evolving relationship between each cryptocurrency and equity market performance

Methodological Rationale: 30-Day Window Selection

Our 30-day rolling window framework balances temporal sensitivity with statistical robustness:

  • Economic Relevance: Approximates one trading month, aligning with quarterly earnings cycles and monthly macroeconomic data releases
  • Signal-to-Noise Optimization: Filters intraday volatility and weekly fluctuations while preserving meaningful medium-term trend information
  • Industry Standard: Consistent with established financial analysis practices for volatility estimation and risk management
  • Event Sensitivity: Responsive enough to capture rapid market regime changes triggered by macro events, policy shifts, or market shocks

The 30-day window achieves optimal balance between granularity and stability. It provides sufficient data points for statistical significance while maintaining agility to detect structural breaks in market relationships—critical for understanding the dynamic interplay between traditional and digital asset markets.

These engineered features form the analytical foundation for all visualizations, enabling direct comparative analysis of volatility profiles, correlation structures, and trend dynamics across crypto and equity markets.
